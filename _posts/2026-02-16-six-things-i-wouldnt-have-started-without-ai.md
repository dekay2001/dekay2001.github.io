## AI is Here and Now

There's a certain kind of task I used to avoid—not because it was impossible, but because the friction felt too high. Drafting legal documents, untangling financial statements, navigating emotional spirals. These weren't things I *couldn't* do, they were things I *wouldn't* start.

AI changed that calculus. Not by doing the work for me, but by making the starting line feel achievable. Here are six things I tackled this past 6 months that I probably would have delayed indefinitely without AI as a thought partner.

### Drafting a Will

I'd been putting this off for years. The combination of morbid subject matter, legal jargon, and not knowing where to start created enough friction that I never moved past "I should really do that someday."

AI helped me work through how to split my assets in a way that actually made sense to me. It explained state-specific requirements, walked me through the decision points I'd need to address, and laid out the concrete steps to formalize and legalize everything. I haven't completed it yet, but I'm no longer avoiding it. I have clarity on what needs to happen, not just vague anxiety about "figure out the will thing." I am confident that I will use AI to create the document and proceed to the next steps.

### Planning a Website

When I wanted to create a community website for a local running club, the old me would have spent weeks researching themes, hosting options, and Jekyll configurations. Or more likely, I would have talked about doing it someday.

Instead, I had a working prototype in minutes. AI helped me think through structure, content, and even generated the initial markdown files. What used to feel like a weekend project became an evening experiment. The barrier dropped from "I need to learn this" to "let's try this now." After three coding sessions, less than two hours each, I have a production ready site.

### Talking Me Off a Ledge

This one surprised me most. I used AI to work through challenging relationship dynamics—how to show up as a leader, how to be a better partner, how to navigate difficult conversations I'd been avoiding.

Through repeated conversations, AI helped me see a pattern in my behavior I hadn't fully recognized. Once I understood it, I couldn't unsee it. That insight forced a decision I'd been skirting around—not because AI told me what to do, but because understanding the "why" made the path forward obvious. It also recommended I pursue deeper therapeutic work to address the roots of this pattern. It's not a replacement for therapy, but it gave me the clarity to make a difficult decision and the self-awareness to know when professional help was needed.

### Physical Therapy & Running Return

I'd been working with physical therapists and a physiatrist for a year and a half. I tried shock therapy, dry needling, massage, took four months completely off running, even gave up my yoga practice. Nothing was fixing the underlying issue that kept me from running.

After long debates with AI about what I was and wasn't doing, it convinced me to focus on something specific at the gym that no one else had mentioned. Within two and a half months, I feel like I can return to running half marathons. I haven't felt this hopeful about running in almost a year and a half. AI didn't replace medical professionals—it synthesized all the information I'd been gathering and identified the missing piece everyone else had overlooked.

### Breaking Down REIT Profitability

I didn't have a good tool for analyzing how my REITs were performing overall—tracking distributions and gains/losses across multiple investments felt overwhelming. The account values had gone down, which was unsettling, but I couldn't tell if I was actually losing money once distributions were factored in.

I copy-pasted my purchase data into AI, and it broke down the actual gains or losses the investments had yielded. Turns out, I was seeing some gains despite the value decline. That clarity gave me comfort. AI didn't just crunch numbers—it helped me understand what the numbers actually meant and whether I should be worried or patient.

### Streamlining To-Dos

My task management system was a mess. Random notes everywhere, duplicated tasks, no clear priorities. I knew the system needed overhaul, but the prospect of organizing the chaos felt paralyzing.

AI helped me dump everything out and structure it back coherently. Not by dictating a system, but by asking questions about how I actually work. What needs daily review? What's project-based? What's just noise? The result wasn't perfect, but it was functional—and that was infinitely better than before.

## The Pattern

These six examples span wildly different domains—legal, technical, emotional, physical, financial, organizational. But they share a common thread: **AI lowered the activation energy.**

Not by doing the work for me. I still drafted the will, built the website, did the physical therapy, made the decisions. But AI transformed "I don't even know where to start" into "okay, let's try this approach."

That shift from paralysis to action is the real story here. The question isn't "what can AI do?" anymore. It's "what have I been avoiding that suddenly feels possible?"
