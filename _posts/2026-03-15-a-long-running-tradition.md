## A Long Running Tradition

A lot of things unfolded this week. For years I've been a member of the Six03 Endurance group in New Hampshire and this week the label officially changed. Personally I am grateful for the relationships and participation I had as a member of Six03 and would not trade it for anything. The group existed long before I started running with them. At first I was only a beginner participant, after the covid pandemic I began becoming a default picture taker and somewhat reluctant leader. After a challenging injury and a winter off starting in September 2024, I came back in the spring of 2025 with a different approach and purpose. Here we are in 2026, with a group of great people, with different goals than the Six03 group, and I find myself in position of leadership under the new banner of Dover Run club.

![Six03 Run](/assets/images/2019-08-28-my-first-six03-run.jpg)

### The Early Days

The image above is my first group photo with the group. There I am looking like a new runner on the right with the red shorts. The guy in the middle wearing a white shirt is the first person I talked to when I arrived and I engaged with him and another woman about the pace and distance I planned to run as I tried to get a feeling for how this run group integration would work. When I told him I was looking for sub 8ish pace, he pointed to the guys sitting in front of me, saying I should stick with the fast guys. It would turn out that I could only hang with them for the first couple miles and ultimately they got faster and my only goal was to keep them in sight so I wouldn't get lost. This was my first running crew with the group, before I knew what the group was about. They are still guys I'm happy to run with from time to time, 7 years later, though I have slowed down a lot, and now I have the privilege of seeing outside of this group.

### Growth and Evolution

Shortly after this, I got to enjoy the privilege of signing up with the group to get some new running clothes and swag that I thought was pretty cool. Christmas and kickoff parties, a bus to watch the Boston Marathon, things were great, but soon after my official membership, COVID hit. Group runs became taboo, but some of us still managed to gather outside the closed restaurant and enjoy some fun runs together. This was the time when I finally started to learn the loops myself. 

Things were never quite the same, but the group did manage to pick back up. There were no more Christmas parties, but the people were still great. Eventually I became a default picture taker at the group when our prior defaults no longer were running with us. Life changes and people grow and move on to their different individual lives. As one of the senior weekly members, I didn't mind. At first I alternated with another member week to week until she had her first kid and couldn't really join us anymore.

In 2023, I started to have some overuse injuries that would eventually cause me to take a break from running in the winter of 2024. By this time I was integrated into multiple run groups and saw some leadership skills start to emerge from people I ran with. I learned quality leadership skills from other group leaders. I wasn't sure I was going to be able to return to running in the same capacity that I had before (this turned out to be the case), but my goal was to get back to it without hurting myself. It wasn't easy addressing my ego when it came to running—staying with the people in the front for the weekly five miles was no longer going to be an option. At least I was able to join the group.

### The Transition to Dover Run Club

When I returned to the group, I decided I would embrace the role of picture taker once again, call out routes, and plan to stay with newer runners in the back to ensure they would make it back. I enjoyed getting to know them and helping them make connections within the group. I noticed there were a lot of supportive sub-groups forming that were very supportive of one another, doing things in addition to runs outside of the group. 

However, I had also heard from people over time that they felt there was cliquishness to the group—and interestingly, I heard this from both older and newer members. I took it upon myself to try to break down some of these barriers by inserting myself into different conversations and happenings within the different groups. Around Christmas time of 2025, after some going-away parties and birthday celebrations, I could see that what I was doing was working. I wanted to invest more time in growing this effort.

In parallel to all this, it became evident that a lot of the group really had no idea Six03 was a thing and didn't know much about it. With fewer benefits being offered, it wasn't straightforward to tell people why they should join. I hadn't joined in the prior year because my injury raised concerns about whether I could even participate. Also, I didn't really need any more swag and shirts.

Rumors hit that the founder of the group was going to potentially sell his races and the Six03 label. I joked with another member in excitement that we could buy it and turn it into something that matched the energy we had. But the truth was, I wasn't interested in the money and I wasn't looking for another job. I just wanted to grow the group we already had and keep doing what we were doing. 

Organically, the group became what it is today. It was clear there was no label or need for us to purchase the label. Since I was also building my leadership skills for my professional career, I was taking interest in AI and learning how to use it and stay current with trends. For fun, I built a website for our new group, and the following week we found out the founder of Six03 had in fact sold all the races and started a new job with the company that bought it. 

Ultimately, for a variety of reasons, we decided within a few months that we would rebrand. We kept it pretty quiet, but when word slipped out within our group, we found a lot of excitement from some of the key members of the Dover Run Club. We decided to start making moves to make this happen. It didn't go as smoothly as we would have liked, but sometimes you have to rip the bandage off so true healing can begin—and so we did.

### What's Next

This entry comes within a week of our first Tuesday run under the Dover Run Club banner, where we had 42 people in attendance. We also had a good turnout for a local race called "Run Before You Crawl," and the group's potential is something I hold dear to my heart at this time. 

My only goal is to continue encouraging people to run, share their passion, and take ownership of the club they are a part of. I hope Six03 continues to be a part of the running community and is welcome to come to our group and take photos in the future. I want to encourage everyone in the group to grow individually and together to make things happen in our community.

## Closing

I have grown a lot from my experience with this group. I found myself in a unique position to be the change I wanted to see within our community, and I had the means and position in the group and in my life to make that happen. I'm not setting out to own or take credit for anything anyone else creates within the group. I hope that someday someone else feels they can take up the mantle and traditions of this long-running tradition—Dover Run Club.
